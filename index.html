Where the work actually starts

I don’t run generic consulting engagements. Teams usually start with a scoped training, strategy, or system design engagement that leads to a concrete next step.

AI training + product strategy

Get the team aligned on what is worth building

Start with half-day workshops, working sessions, or a short discovery sprint that moves AI from vague interest to real priorities.

Use this stage to align on the workflow, the user, the constraint set, and what not to build before execution starts.

Applied AI systems

Turn the idea into a system people can actually use

Shape copilots, workflows, internal tools, and operating models around what can ship in weeks and work in practice.

This stage usually ends with a system design, execution path, success criteria, and a working prototype direction.

GTM + team enablement

Make sure the work lands with the team and the market

Support rollout, team adoption, messaging, ICP alignment, and launch structure so the work does not stall after delivery.

The goal is not just a shipped feature. It is a system the team can run with, explain clearly, and extend without outside dependence.
